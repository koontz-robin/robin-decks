July 9, 2026
Pre-demo sync with Toni
Participants included Rev.io sales leadership and Spectrotel. AirSpring context was discussed for the upcoming KPMG evaluation demo.
Summary

The call framed the Spectrotel/AirSpring merger as a Charles Bank-backed combination where KPMG will evaluate systems applications, with billing under the highest scrutiny. The group aligned that Rev.io should first demonstrate Spectrotel's current-state environment, then broaden into capabilities Spectrotel is not using today, and only then discuss new Rev.io/roadmap.

  • KPMG, Sanjay, Mitch Wright, and John Moss were identified as critical evaluation stakeholders.
  • AirSpring currently uses RiverRock and appears to have highly manual, customer-specific billing customization.
  • AirSpring has usage billing, weekly billing, circuit billing, voice/long-distance usage, CAD/EUR billing, and a small mobility component.
  • Scalability is the top technical concern: NuCo demand is expected to be 2x at migration completion, with CDR processing likely growing faster than overall company growth.
  • The IMO appears to prefer keeping NuCo billing in Rev.io if Rev.io can prove scale, migration discipline, and AirSpring-specific billing fit.
  • The recommended demo flow is current-state Rev.io first, broader readiness/capacity second, and new Rev.io or roadmap last, potentially in a second session.

Rev.io Fit

Rev.io's strongest argument is continuity plus modernization

The transcript repeatedly points to a practical thesis: keep what is already working at Spectrotel, prove Rev.io can absorb AirSpring, and use the merger to activate capabilities Spectrotel has deferred.

Recommended Evaluation Flow

Two-hour demo structure from the transcript

The group converged on a staged demo: current Spectrotel state first, broader Rev.io capabilities second, and new Rev.io third.

First Hour
Current-state Rev.io, not new Rev.io
Start with Spectrotel's current Rev.io environment. Limit "this will be different in new Rev.io" comments during this section so KPMG and AirSpring can compare current state cleanly.
>
Next 30 Minutes
Broader Rev.io readiness and capacity
Cover scalability, capacity planning, availability, BCDR, infrastructure readiness, integration flexibility, internationalization, usage rating, and mobility.
>
Last, or Session 2
New Rev.io roadmap and platform vision
Move roadmap and new Rev.io last, and consider making it a second session if time is tight. That is where current-state differences should be highlighted.
The demo should not let AirSpring's listen-mode status hide their requirements. Even if AirSpring does not actively participate, the Rev.io team should proactively show capabilities that map to AirSpring's known billing patterns: usage rating, multi-currency, weekly or high-frequency billing considerations, product catalog governance, and migration validation.
Open Questions

Information still needed before KPMG goes deep

These are the follow-ups the team should chase before the formal evaluation so Rev.io can answer with evidence instead of broad confidence.

AirSpring Data and Billing
Clarify the actual migration surface
Confirm AirSpring product catalog quality, customer-level customization, bill-cycle structure, weekly billing scope, high-usage controls, tax rules, invoice formats, current RiverRock data exports, circuit billing, and whether mobility/prepaid is material or marginal.
Stakeholders
Map who can influence the recommendation
Confirm AirSpring attendees beyond Shannon, KPMG decision participants, Sanjay's technical proof requirements, John Moss's evaluation role, Mitch Wright's specific multi-currency acceptance criteria, and the power users who identified RiverRock slowness/manual work.
Technical Proof
Bring evidence to the scalability question
Prepare infrastructure architecture, transaction-volume benchmarks, bill-run timing examples, test-data approach, monitoring controls, BCDR/availability proof, and escalation resources who can speak credibly to platform performance.
Migration Strategy
Avoid premature certainty on the path
Keep the migration recommendation open until discovery is complete: AirSpring into current Spectrotel Rev.io first, direct-to-new Rev.io where possible, or phased capability activation depending on data quality and timeline.
Department Workflows
Identify billing touchpoints outside finance
The notes ask how implementation, service delivery, and sales update data or initiate workflows in the billing platform at both companies. Those users and handoffs need to be mapped before migration design.
Integrations and Commissions
Confirm platform dependencies
Identify proprietary and third-party integrations in the AirSpring stack, whether agent commissions run from the incumbent platform, and how DAX/order management connects to billing today.
Bottom line: the opportunity is strong because Spectrotel's IMO appears to prefer Rev.io, RiverRock does not appear to have strong advocacy, and a brand-new platform would threaten the integration timeline. The risk is that KPMG will require proof on scale, migration discipline, and AirSpring-specific billing complexity. The evaluation summary should therefore focus less on generic feature coverage and more on de-risking NuCo's operating model.
