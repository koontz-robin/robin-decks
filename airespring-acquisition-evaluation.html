Evaluation Risks

The decision will be won or lost on risk reduction

KPMG is expected to look under the hood. The demo needs to answer operational risk, not just show product screens.

Risk 1
Infrastructure and transaction scale
Sanjay's top concern is whether Rev.io can support NuCo as revenue and transaction volume roughly double, then continue scaling through future tuck-in acquisitions.
Needs proof
Risk 2
Internationalization and currencies
AirSpring bills in Canadian dollars and euros. International revenue was estimated at 5-6%, but multi-currency support is important enough that Mitch Wright is worried about it.
Visible concern
Risk 3
AirSpring billing complexity
AirSpring does significant usage billing, mostly voice and long distance, with some weekly billing for high-usage customers to manage arrears exposure.
Complex use case
Risk 4
Customer-level customization
The IMO is concerned AirSpring may have customization down to the customer level, potentially including weak product catalog discipline. That could make migration, reporting, and revenue categorization difficult.
Migration hazard
Risk 5
Current UI perception
KPMG may view the current Rev.io environment as dated if the demo only shows Spectrotel's existing instance. The evaluation needs to connect current reliability with the modern new Rev.io experience.
Positioning risk
Risk 6
Rip-and-replace alternative
There has been discussion about moving NuCo to a completely new billing platform. The IMO is not voting for that because it would materially extend the integration timeline, but KPMG may still evaluate it.
Competitive risk
Rev.io Fit

Rev.io's strongest argument is continuity plus modernization

The transcript repeatedly points to a practical thesis: keep what is already working at Spectrotel, prove Rev.io can absorb AirSpring, and use the merger to activate capabilities Spectrotel has deferred.

Evaluation Area Transcript Signal Rev.io Positioning
Billing consolidationNuCo needs one platform decision. IMO preference is to keep all billing in Rev.io if concerns can be resolved. Rev.io is the least disruptive path because Spectrotel already operates on it and the team understands the environment.
Usage billingAirSpring has meaningful voice usage billing. Majority of usage billing appears to be voice and long distance; weekly billing exists for some high-usage customers. Lead with usage rating, bill-cycle handling, high-volume processing, and controls for exposure management.

New Rev.ioThe modern platform needs careful naming. The team agreed to move away from calling it PSA and refer to it as new Rev.io. Show shared backend continuity, modern UI, AI capabilities, stronger order/service management, and lower migration friction.
Recommended Evaluation Flow

Two-hour demo structure from the transcript

The group converged on a staged demo: current Spectrotel state first, broader Rev.io capabilities second, and new Rev.io third.

First Hour
Current Spectrotel Rev.io environment
Show what Spectrotel owns and uses today. Establish current-state credibility before jumping into future-state capabilities.
>
Next 30 Minutes
Broader Rev.io billing capabilities
Use a Rev.io demo environment to cover internationalization, usage rating, mobility, infrastructure, and scale not fully visible in Spectrotel's instance.
>
Final 30 Minutes
New Rev.io platform vision
Present modern UI, AI-native workflows, order/service management, and the shared backend story that reduces migration friction.
The demo should not let AirSpring's listen-mode status hide their requirements. Even if AirSpring does not actively participate, the Rev.io team should proactively show capabilities that map to AirSpring's known billing patterns: usage rating, multi-currency, weekly or high-frequency billing considerations, product catalog governance, and migration validation.
Open Questions

Information still needed before KPMG goes deep

These are the follow-ups the team should chase before the formal evaluation so Rev.io can answer with evidence instead of broad confidence.

AirSpring Data and Billing
Clarify the actual migration surface
Confirm AirSpring product catalog quality, customer-level customization, bill-cycle structure, weekly billing scope, high-usage controls, tax rules, invoice formats, current RiverRock data exports, and whether mobility/prepaid is material or marginal.
Stakeholders
Map who can influence the recommendation
Confirm AirSpring attendees beyond Shannon, KPMG decision participants, Sanjay's technical proof requirements, John Moss's evaluation role, and Mitch Wright's specific multi-currency acceptance criteria.
Technical Proof
Bring evidence to the scalability question
Prepare infrastructure architecture, transaction-volume benchmarks, bill-run timing examples, test-data approach, monitoring controls, and escalation resources who can speak credibly to platform performance.
Migration Strategy
Avoid premature certainty on the path
Keep the migration recommendation open until discovery is complete: AirSpring into current Spectrotel Rev.io first, direct-to-new Rev.io where possible, or phased capability activation depending on data quality and timeline.
Bottom line: the opportunity is strong because Spectrotel's IMO appears to prefer Rev.io, RiverRock does not appear to have strong advocacy, and a brand-new platform would threaten the integration timeline. The risk is that KPMG will require proof on scale, migration discipline, and AirSpring-specific billing complexity. The evaluation summary should therefore focus less on generic feature coverage and more on de-risking NuCo's operating model.
