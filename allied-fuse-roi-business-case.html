Why Rev.io

Fuse needs more than a billing replacement. It needs a consolidated Rev.io Billing + PSA platform.

The executive message is not that Rev.io is simply another tool. Rev.io replaces unsupported billing risk and fragmented workflows with a supported platform where billing, customer records, service work, onboarding, and reporting can operate from the same foundation.

01
Telecom billing depth
Rev.io Billing is built for communications providers that need account hierarchy, service-level detail, invoice controls, collections workflows, and customer-facing billing clarity.
02
Rev.io PSA is built around billing
Unlike general PSA tools that sit beside the billing engine, Rev.io PSA connects service operations to the same customer and billing foundation Fuse needs to modernize.
03
Workflow control from quote to cash
Rev.io can align onboarding, billing start readiness, service handoffs, collections follow-up, and reporting so teams are not reconciling work across disconnected systems.
04
One operating stack
Billing and PSA together reduce double entry, improve visibility, and give leadership a clearer operating model than a billing replacement plus a separate PSA sidecar.
Business Issue

Fuse's current problems are bigger than support risk.

Fuse's current operating model points to a fragmented billing operation: account hierarchy gaps, payment-plan workarounds, manual billing-start tracking, bilingual invoice needs, VIP customer handling, SQL reporting, and duplicate entry into Synchro Team.

If Freeside breaks, billing continuity depends on a retired developer and a codebase with limited active maintenance.

Core business risk for leadership alignment
Unsupported Freeside platform
The original developer retired and the codebase receives limited active maintenance, leaving billing continuity dependent on a fragile support path.
Billing continuity risk
Disconnected customer entry
Customer data is manually entered across Freeside and Synchro Team, creating duplicate work and a higher risk of setup errors.
Duplicate system work
No parent-child account structure
Larger multi-location invoices become disorganized, and wholesale internet relationships cannot be cleanly tied together.
Account hierarchy gap
SQL-only custom reporting
Custom reporting requires manual SQL queries instead of a report builder business users can own.
Visibility gap
Payment and collections workarounds
Partial payments, promises to pay, payment plans, and balance thresholds require manual tracking outside the core billing system.
Collections friction
Invoice and service-detail limitations
Fuse needs bilingual invoice profiles, service-type breakout, billing start date control, VIP flags, and cleaner customer-facing billing detail.
Customer experience gap

Why Now?

Fuse has outgrown a billing stack that still runs, but no longer scales or protects the business.

This is not a feature-shopping exercise. The timing is about replacing unsupported billing dependency, reducing manual operating work, and choosing a platform that can support Billing first, PSA next, and unified-stack benefits together.

Current state
Unsupported platform plus fragmented operations
No support: Freeside failure risk sits directly in the billing cycle.
Disconnected hierarchy: Parent, child, wholesale, and end-client relationships are hard to represent.
Manual operations: Customer entry, billing start dates, partial payments, and VIP flags need workarounds.
SQL reporting: Leaders wait on technical users for custom answers.

Consolidated Rev.io PSA + Billing
Billing continuity: Supported communications billing platform replacing Freeside.
Unified data: Billing and PSA in one database to reduce duplicate entry and disconnected handoffs.

Automated workflows: Onboarding, quote-to-cash, promise-to-pay, and collections workflows in-system.
Reporting: Billing and operational visibility without relying only on manual SQL work.
