Migration scope

Data AVIT should expect Rev.io to move cleanly.

The first implementation risk is not configuration. It is moving the right ConnectWise data while keeping stale CRM noise out of the new operating system.

Customers

Account foundation

Company name, ConnectWise account ID, service/billing/listing/site addresses, account creation date, and active status.

Contacts

Clean contact model

Full name, title, phone, email, primary contact designation, active status, and selective migration rules for stale contacts.

Financial history

Invoices, payments, credits

Invoice number, date, due date, amounts by service/expense/product, credit memos, payments, and outstanding balances. Voided invoices stay out.

Product catalog

SKU and service catalog

Products with description, rate, cost, status, classification, subcategory, manufacturer, unit of measure, serialized flags, and part numbers.

Tickets

PSA history

Description, type, status, priority, severity, opened/closed dates, customer association, and configured reference values.

Notes

Communication record

All ticket notes linked to migrated tickets so communication history survives cutover and remains searchable.

AVIT operating model

Why this needs a custom plan.

AVIT is not a simple PSA swap. NetSuite, franchise billing, field workflows, subcontractors, CSP/distributors, and reporting all collide at go-live.

NetSuite gravity

Financial and inventory source of truth

Rev.io must respect NetSuite boundaries while improving invoice handoff, product visibility, and ticket-level inventory context.

Field workforce

Simple execution for techs and subcontractors

Mobile access, photos, notes, time, signatures, GPS context, and one-time work links need to be validated before cutover.

Franchise complexity

Corporate, owner, location, and sibling relationships

Recurring billing and break-fix billing have to support real-world hospitality account structures, not a toy parent-child demo.

Workflow intensity

Automation without losing billing control

AVIT needs triage logic, SLA rules, agreement profitability, billing review, project change orders, and operational dashboards.

Data blueprint

Extract, transform, validate. No mystery meat migration.

The plan is designed to make quality measurable: record counts, exception logs, reference-value mapping, sample loads, and formal sign-off.

1 · Extract

Pull from ConnectWise and NetSuite context

Companies, contacts, tickets, notes, invoices, payments, credits, products, agreements, project records, and NetSuite references.

2 · Transform

Normalize AVIT’s structure

Map parent/child/related-account relationships, statuses, priorities, types, billing rules, and stale contact suppression logic.

3 · Validate

Make AVIT sign-off explicit

Sample loads, full loads, invoice reconciliation, ticket/note counts, product catalog checks, open-ticket review, and exception acceptance.
