June 4 Onsite Transcript Analysis

AVIT validated the Rev.io thesis, but the win depends on execution against complex workflow gaps.

Across the onsite sessions, AVIT reacted positively to Rev.io's billing DNA, reporting/AI direction, mobile/service workflows, API-first posture, and partnership transparency. The biggest risks are not generic PSA fit. They are specific: NetSuite, integrations, quote/inventory depth, subcontractor access, and roadmap credibility.

Executive Read

AVIT is not evaluating whether Rev.io is a better-looking ConnectWise. They are testing whether Rev.io can become their operating layer.

The strongest company-wide takeaway: AVIT is center-of-fairway for the PSA strategy, but they are unusually complex. They need a partner that will co-design around hospitality, AV, IT, telecom, NetSuite, high-volume service, long projects, and subcontractors.

"The Lego blocks are already built. We just need to connect them together." Theme from the service workflow discussion: many asks are adjacent to current platform capabilities, but need productized workflow depth.
Core Risk

Transparency is the buying criterion.

AVIT repeatedly contrasted Rev.io's candor with Kaseya/ConnectWise roadmap opacity. Any vague answer on integrations, roadmap dates, migration scope, or current-state gaps will hurt more than admitting a gap.

Positive Feedback

What landed well with AVIT

These were the clearest signs of product-market fit and sales messaging that should be shared internally.

ICP Fit

"Center of the fairway"

The team explicitly positioned AVIT as the exact kind of customer Rev.io is designing for: low-voltage, IT, telecom, hospitality, security, complex billing, and high operational volume.

Reporting

Built-in BrightGauge alternative

SQL lab, chat-with-data, shareable links, auto-refresh, alerts, and dashboard creation were received as meaningful internal BI value, not a cosmetic feature.

AI

Revy felt practical

Transcript cleanup, voice-driven time entries, ticket actions, analytics querying, and agentic workflows were framed as adoption drivers, especially for field users and managers.

Mobile

Clear operational win

Offline access, GPS/geofencing, photos, video, push notifications, work/travel timers, signatures, and ticket history directly address the PDF-ticket and TSheets workaround.

Billing DNA

Telecom-grade billing resonated

Mixed cadence billing, recurring services, tax complexity, invoice templates, parent/related-account flexibility, and agreement profitability all mapped to AVIT's complexity.

API First

Developer portal and API keys matter

Unlimited API-style flexibility, API key management, and a developer portal contrast well against ConnectWise constraints and AVIT's large integration estate.

Agreement Direction

Consolidation is a differentiator

AVIT wants one agreement with multiple components, billing cycles, coverage rules, overages, and renewal intelligence instead of agreement sprawl.

Service UX

Ticket views and notes were credible

Configurable boards, shared/private views, tags, custom fields, macros, activity notes, and Revise addressed everyday service desk work.

Partnership

The conversation created trust

Rev.io's willingness to say what is live, what is coming, and what needs more work was itself a positive signal for a buyer frustrated by vendor opacity.

Software Gaps

Where we said, implied, or exposed that the product is not fully there today

These should not be hidden. They are the areas Product and Sales need crisp current-state language, owner, date, and fallback plan for.

NetSuite

Real-time inventory at ticket/quote level is not live

Pulling on-hand inventory from NetSuite into service and quoting views is the largest "wow" requirement, but current language was feasibility through API/custom build, not existing production capability.

Billing Automation

Automation is still too all-or-nothing

Invoicing can automate charges, invoice creation, and sending, but the current model lacks enough selective controls for AVIT's review-heavy billing process.

Agreements

Block-time rollover and expiration need work

The team captured rollover/expiration as a fast follow. AVIT's support models need block time, covered work, uncovered work, role/reason-code nuance, and overage handling in one structure.

Expenses

Shipping, PO, and exception review are immature

AVIT needs better automation around vendor cost, customer billing, FedEx/shipping charges, and mismatched billable items. AI-assisted exception detection is a needed direction.

Quote Depth

Inventory costing and quote margin are incomplete

They asked for supplier pricing, quantity on hand, suggested replacements, and quote profitability/margin approvals. Current quote costing does not fully flow through.

Quote Experience

Images, optional lines, and view alerts remain roadmap

Default product images, optional line items, quote-view email alerts, and stronger product-catalog image behavior are important, with images specifically flagged as a deal-breaker risk.

Project Billing

Quote-to-project and progressive billing need deeper automation

Today quotes can create tickets and pending recurring services. AVIT's long construction projects need direct quote-to-project, deposit, change-order, and milestone billing flows.

Workflow Engine

Triage and macros are not yet smart enough

Email routing, quote-created tickets, and macros exist, but AVIT wants logic-driven dispatch, 911 prioritization, reminders, SLA pause rules, and Revy-informed triage.

Portal Access

One-off subcontractor access does not exist as requested

The ideal generated link for subs with time in/out, notes, photos, GPS check-in, customer sign-off, and no account creation is not current product. It became a prototype action item.

VIP Routing

SLA/contact-role handling is account-centric today

AVIT needs role-aware routing for manager-on-duty, brand ambassador, owner, VIP, and special-contact scenarios. Current SLA logic links primarily to accounts/status/workflows.

Integrations

More pre-built vendor support is needed

SonicWall, Meraki, TD Synnex, Ingram, IT Glue, Field Nation, ServiceNow/Jira continuity, and NetSuite depth all need clear status and prioritization.

Bulk Data UX

Bulk address import and selective migration are open issues

Multi-address quoting is supported, but bulk address import was missing. Migration also needs selective import/scrubbing instead of a noisy everything-over approach.

Lightbulbs & Feature Suggestions

Ideas worth moving into Product discovery

These are not all AVIT-only. Several are broadly applicable to MSPs, integrators, telecom providers, and high-volume service organizations.

Portal

Subcontractor sign-off link

Generate a ticket-specific link that captures time in/out, notes, signer name, signature, GPS, and photos without requiring a Rev.io user account.

Service

VIP / role-based routing

Route and escalate by contact role or business role, such as manager on duty, brand ambassador, owner, VIP, or support level, not only by account.

AI Ops

AI-assisted billing exception review

Use AI to find mismatched expenses, missing shipping charges, vendor/customer cost deltas, and items that look billable but were not added to invoice review.

CRM

Lead stage before opportunity

Capture word-of-mouth referrals earlier, before formal opportunity creation, and track which referrals convert versus stall.

Quoting

Inventory-aware quote lines

Show on-hand quantity, source system, replacement suggestions, supplier availability, cost, and margin while quoting.

Reporting

Permanent operational dashboards

Lean into always-current, auto-refresh dashboards for service queues, aging tickets, billable work not invoiced, and agreement margin alerts.

Account Alerts

Actionable pop-ups and reminders

Extend account/ticket alerts for credit holds, unopened restaurants, special projects, customer status, follow-up reminders, and bulk location updates.

Integrations

Fallback data layer

Copy critical IT Glue/configuration data into Rev.io so teams can keep working when external systems or internet access are degraded.

Agreements

Package billing with hidden component detail

Let customers see a clean package total while the backend keeps component-level charges, cost, tax, and reconciliation detail.

Challenges Uncovered

Why AVIT is a hard but valuable customer

These are the operational realities that make AVIT more complex than a standard MSP migration.

Business Model

They are a systems integrator plus MSP plus telecom provider

Hospitality, restaurants, AV, IT, security, surveillance, VoIP, signage, white-label help desk, and field service all overlap in the same operating model.

NetSuite

NetSuite is the source of truth

Inventory, catalog, deferred revenue, invoicing, payment flow, and invoice-number continuity all depend on NetSuite. Rev.io cannot treat it as a peripheral integration.

Scale

High service volume

100,000+ annual tickets and dispatch handling 15-20 calls per hour mean small UI or workflow inefficiencies become expensive fast.

Projects

9-18 month construction projects

Projects start before ground break, use deposits and pre-purchased hardware, and require inventory reservation, change orders, and milestone/progressive billing.

Subcontractors

70% of subs may be one-off

Full user accounts do not make sense for workers who touch one job once. AVIT needs accountable access without account administration overhead.

Data

Migration is also cleanup

ConnectWise and NetSuite data are split, stale relationship data exists, Catalyst syncs nightly, ticket sync is off, and selective scrubbing may be better than blind import.

Users

Licensing has shaped broken workflows

Only about 70 users are in ConnectWise today, but 130 need access. PDF tickets through Teams and TSheets time tracking exist partly because licensing/mobile adoption failed.

Competitive

Halo's integration count is the obvious risk

Halo's 250+ integration story must be countered with depth: NetSuite API, custom integration partnership, billing strength, AI, and roadmap influence.

Timing

They will not rush

December 2026 is the renewal trigger, but AVIT is open to a longer, phased migration and will avoid holiday blackout. A credible timeline beats a compressed one.

Recommended Internal Actions

What should happen next

This is the practical follow-up list for the company, not just the sales team.

Product

Create an AVIT gap tracker

One owner per gap: NetSuite inventory, subcontractor link, quote images, quote margin, workflow triage, block time, package billing, and vendor integrations.

Engineering

Scope NetSuite as a go-live dependency

Define exactly what is live, custom, and future: invoice push, invoice-number matching, on-hand inventory, product catalog, deferred revenue, payments, and purchasing.

Sales

Prepare transparent current-state language

For every roadmap feature, document what we can show today, what is committed, estimated release timing, and a workaround if AVIT needs it before release.

CS / Onboarding

Build a phased migration narrative

Cover data scrub, integration rebuild, parallel run, open tickets/projects, active agreements, user training, and holiday blackout constraints.

Leadership

Treat AVIT as roadmap-shaping

Their asks are not edge cases. They expose what high-complexity integrators need from the PSA strategy.

Marketing

Capture the new buyer story

Rev.io is strongest when positioned as billing-native, AI-enabled, API-first, transparent, and willing to co-build around complex operations.
