Current State Pain

What ConnectWise is actually costing AVIT

These aren't hypothetical problems — they came straight from AVIT's leadership in discovery.

🔒
Field Tech Access Blocked
AVIT wants 130 users on the platform. They're capped at 70 because ConnectWise's per-seat pricing makes it cost-prohibitive to give field techs access. Workaround: QuickBooks T-sheets + Microsoft Teams messages.
"I have technicians that don't have access that I want to have access... that's a limitation because of their terrible software."
60 users × $109/mo = $78K/yr gap
🌳
Broken Parent-Child Hierarchy
AVIT's hospitality clients are complex franchise structures — corporate billing for recurring, location billing for break-fix. ConnectWise's "simplistic 90s view of parent-child" forces manual workarounds every billing cycle.
"It's a very simplistic 90s view of parent-child."
Hours of manual reconciliation/month
🔗
No Native NetSuite Integration
AVIT migrated to NetSuite in Jan 2024 for accounting + HR. They need parts/labor from tickets to flow directly into NetSuite. ConnectWise requires custom middleware — another integration to manage in their 75-endpoint stack.
"Parts and labor integration with NetSuite for tickets [is needed]."
Custom dev + ongoing maintenance
📄
No Subcontractor Portal
AVIT uses third-party subcontractors on installs and needs digital customer sign-offs — without issuing full platform licenses. Today: paper forms. TigerPaw had a free subcontractor portal. ConnectWise doesn't.
"I want a third party subcontractor to be able to get a customer sign off without having to print out a piece of paper."
Operational inefficiency + liability risk
📝
CPQ Complexity + Change Orders
AVIT regularly generates multi-million dollar proposals with variable pricing, tabbed-out proposals, and the need to hide MSRP. Change order management is critical. ConnectWise CPQ is rigid and doesn't flex to their deal structure.
"Numerous trials and making modifications to support multi-million dollar proposals."
Lost deal velocity
🚀
Platform Stagnation
AVIT's leadership is watching ConnectWise fall behind on AI and innovation. The company was partly sold on ASIO — which has since stalled. They're actively evaluating everything: PSA, RMM, "anything and everything in between."
"I am going to be looking at anything and everything when it comes to PSA, RMM... I want to be not playing this game come November of next year."
Strategic risk of staying
📊
Limited Profitability Visibility
AVIT cannot easily see agreement profitability, multi-location cost allocation, or customer-level margins without custom exports and reporting workarounds.
Agreement profitability surfaced as a critical pain point in the second demo.
Margin leakage hidden until too late
🧩
Integration Sprawl
ConnectWise, NetSuite, IT Glue, Paystand, Nextiva, CSP providers, distributors, ServiceNow, Jira, and field workflows are stitched together across dozens of connectors.
Every connector is another maintenance point and another reconciliation risk.
Operational drag across billing cycles
🛰️
No Real-Time Field Visibility
Dispatchers lack real-time visibility into field tech location, route, and job status. Phone, text, Teams, and TSheets become the operating model.
Time has to be reconnected back to tickets for billing after the work is done.
Duplicate entry + billing errors

Migration Journey

A phased path from ConnectWise to a unified operating system

The updated onsite deck frames the move in two phases: first replace ConnectWise Manage as the PSA system of record, then consolidate NetSuite and integration workflows around Rev.io's open API.

Phase I
ConnectWise Migration
Today

→
Rev.io PSA

Phase II
NetSuite Consolidation
Today
NetSuite plus 75 integration endpoints, CSP reconciliation, distributors, and custom middleware

Unified Flow
