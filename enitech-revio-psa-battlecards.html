Problem with generic PSA evaluations

Where Rev.io changes the frame

Revenue Workflow Becomes the Center

Why it matters

Every spreadsheet, billing workaround, processor handoff, and reconciliation step creates delay, leakage, and noise. Rev.io wins when those operating costs are visible.

Section 3

Competitive Battlecards

Do not attack Halo or Autotask as bad products. Treat them as credible PSAs, then force the conversation into the areas where Rev.io is materially different.

Rev.io PSA vs Halo PSA

Halo is a serious PSA option, but the Rev.io play is to challenge whether Halo is enough when the buying need includes billing, payments, recurring revenue, and multi-service quote-to-cash.

Where Rev.io Applies Pressure
  • Native billing strength for recurring, one-time, and usage-style revenue.
  • Payments and collections connected to the operational workflow.
  • Better fit for MSPs selling beyond standard managed IT services.
  • Stronger quote-to-cash story from opportunity through payment.
  • Rev.io's roadmap and implementation partnership can be shaped around high-fit operating needs.
Where Halo Can Be Dangerous
  • Buyers who only evaluate core ticketing workflows may not feel Rev.io's full value.
  • Halo's broad MSP ecosystem can make it look safer in checklist-driven evaluations.
  • If Enitech does not expose billing, payment, or reconciliation pain, Halo gets to stay in a service-desk-only comparison.

Halo may check PSA boxes, but the real question is what system owns the work after the ticket: agreements, recurring charges, invoice accuracy, payment collection, and revenue visibility. Rev.io is the stronger fit if Enitech wants the PSA to run the business, not just the ticket queue.

Rev.io PSA vs Autotask

Autotask is a mature MSP PSA, especially inside the Kaseya/Datto ecosystem. The sales move is to separate mature ticketing from a flexible revenue operations platform.

Where Rev.io Applies Pressure
  • Less dependency on the Kaseya/Datto bundle and its commercial lock-in dynamics.
  • Modern operating flow across sales, service, billing, and collections.
  • Stronger billing and payments narrative when revenue operations matter.
  • Better fit for providers that do not want to stay boxed into pure managed IT workflows.
  • Executive-level value story around leakage, margin, cash collection, and operational scale.
Where Autotask Can Be Dangerous
  • Datto RMM, IT Glue, and Kaseya stack tie-ins can create a sticky default choice.
  • Autotask is known and mature, which can feel lower-risk to conservative buyers.
  • Bundle pricing can distract from the total cost of workarounds, reconciliation, and lock-in.

Autotask is a familiar choice if Enitech wants a traditional MSP PSA. The risk is ending up with mature ticketing but still needing separate processes for billing, payment collection, and revenue reporting. Rev.io gives Enitech a cleaner operating model from quote to cash.

Section 4

Discovery Questions That Change the Deal

These questions force the competitive conversation out of feature-list theater and into operating pain. Use them early.

What triggered the PSA evaluation now?
Are Halo and Autotask active finalists, or just names on the shortlist?
What has to improve most: ticketing, billing, projects, reporting, payments, or all of it?
After a quote is approved, how many systems are involved before the client is billed and payment is collected?
Where does work leave the PSA and move into spreadsheets, QuickBooks, portals, or manual reconciliation?
If service desk functionality is comparable, who wins on billing, collections, and recurring revenue operations?
What services do you sell today that do not fit neatly into standard managed IT billing?
Where do billing errors, late invoices, missed renewals, or uncollected balances show up today?
Rep Note

If the buyer only talks about tickets, ask what happens before and after the ticket. If they talk about billing, collections, recurring revenue, or manual reconciliation, Rev.io has the wedge.
