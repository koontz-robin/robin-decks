Sales Thesis

Lead with Rev.io PSA as the revenue operations PSA, not just another service desk. Halo and Autotask are credible, but force the decision around whether Enitech can run quote, agreement, service delivery, recurring billing, invoice, payment, and reporting in one operating motion.

Rev.io wins when the pain is quote-to-cash, billing accuracy, payment collection, recurring revenue, and reducing manual handoffs. If the buying criteria collapses to pure ticketing UI or Kaseya stack stickiness, Halo and Autotask become more dangerous.

Battlecard 1

Rev.io PSA vs Halo PSA

Halo is a strong modern PSA. Do not dismiss it. Acknowledge the UI, ticketing experience, and integration story, then pivot to billing, payments, and revenue operations.

Where Rev.io Wins

  • Native billing strength for recurring, one-time, and usage-style revenue.
  • Integrated payment and collection workflow instead of separate processor reconciliation.
  • Better fit for MSPs selling beyond standard managed IT: cloud, voice, internet, security, and bundled services.
  • Cleaner quote-to-cash story from opportunity to quote to agreement to invoice to payment.
  • Roadmap partnership angle for a high-fit customer instead of a generic PSA implementation lane.

Where Halo Is Dangerous

  • Modern UI and strong service desk reputation.
  • All-inclusive pricing story.
  • Broad MSP ecosystem and integration momentum.
  • Buyers focused on ITIL, asset/change management, ticketing polish, and technician UX.

Discovery Landmines

  1. If you choose Halo, what system owns billing and payment collection?
  2. How many tools are involved between quote approval and cash collected?
  3. Do you need one invoice across managed IT plus other recurring services?
  4. What revenue leakage or manual reconciliation exists after tickets or projects are completed?
  5. Are you evaluating PSA features, or the full customer lifecycle from sale to service to invoice to payment?

Talk Track

Halo is probably the cleanest-looking PSA in the conversation, and if this were purely a ticketing UI decision, I would take them seriously. Where Rev.io separates is after the ticket: agreements, billing, payment collection, and multi-service revenue.

Objection Handling

Halo has better UI.
Fair. Halo is strong there. But UI does not collect cash, reconcile invoices, or handle complex recurring revenue. If the business problem is bigger than ticketing, Rev.io deserves the deeper look.
Halo includes everything.
Included features only matter if they solve the full workflow. The question is whether Halo includes the billing, payment, and revenue automation Enitech actually needs.
Battlecard 2

Rev.io PSA vs Autotask

Autotask is mature and sticky, especially inside Kaseya/Datto. Separate mature ticketing from modern revenue operations.

Where Rev.io Wins

  • Less Kaseya dependency and fewer bundle/contract lock-in concerns.
  • Modern operating flow versus a legacy MSP stack.
  • Stronger billing and payments story when recurring revenue and collections matter.
  • More flexibility for providers that do not want to stay boxed into pure managed IT workflows.
  • Executive conversation around margin, cash collection, revenue leakage, and operational scale.

Where Autotask Is Dangerous

  • Strong Datto RMM, IT Glue, and broader Kaseya ecosystem tie-ins.
  • Mature service desk and reporting.
  • Kaseya bundle economics may look attractive upfront.
  • If Enitech already lives in Kaseya and values integration over flexibility, Autotask can feel like the default.

Discovery Landmines

  1. Are you already standardized on Kaseya/Datto, or trying to avoid more lock-in?
  2. How many steps happen after a quote is approved before the client is billed and payment is collected?
  3. Where does billing break when services are recurring, bundled, or non-standard?
  4. Do technicians like the PSA enough to keep time and notes current?
  5. Is the buying priority ticketing maturity, or reducing manual work across sales, service, billing, and collections?

Talk Track

Autotask is a safe-looking choice if Enitech wants a traditional MSP PSA, especially inside Kaseya. The risk is ending up with a mature ticketing system but still needing separate processes for billing, payments, and revenue operations.

Objection Handling

Autotask is proven.
Agreed. Autotask is proven for service desk. The decision is whether proven ticketing solves Enitech's broader operating model: quote-to-cash, billing accuracy, and reducing manual revenue work.
The Kaseya bundle is cheaper.
It may be cheaper on a line-item basis. The real comparison is total cost: PSA plus billing tools, payment processing, reconciliation labor, contract risk, and the cost of workarounds.

Recommended Discovery Path

  1. Start broad: What triggered the PSA evaluation now?
  2. Identify competitor shape: Are Halo and Autotask active options, or just names on the shortlist?
  3. Force business criteria: What has to improve: ticketing, billing, projects, reporting, payments, or all of it?
  4. Quantify manual work: Where does work leave the PSA and move into spreadsheets, QuickBooks, portals, or manual reconciliation?
  5. Split the decision: If service desk is equal, who wins on billing, collections, and recurring revenue operations?
