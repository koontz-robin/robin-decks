Four phases of Revii adoption.

Each phase creates business value without asking Intermedia to bet the entire migration on day one.

Phase 01

AI visibility before migration

Revii starts by helping Intermedia understand the current state across Atlas, BRM, HostPilot, CDRs, and SKU exports.

Summarize billing exceptions
Find reconciliation mismatches
Flag stale SKUs and duplicate pricing
Phase 02

Rev.io as the billing engine

Rev.io takes over charge generation, rating, usage, tax-ready data, and invoice XML while HostPilot stays customer-facing.

Generate charges centrally
Process PSTN CDRs and overages
Feed HostPilot with validated invoice data
Phase 03

Revenue assurance with Revii

Once Rev.io owns more billing data, Revii watches for leakage, margin compression, missing usage, and pricing drift.

Detect usage without charges
Compare wholesale and retail logic
Surface distributor margin risk
Phase 04

Move tribal logic into Rev.io

Legacy billing rules become documented, searchable, testable, and configurable instead of living in custom code and engineering memory.

Translate BRM rules into Rev.io logic
Create migration QA checklists
Build institutional billing memory

What Intermedia teams could ask Revii.

The use case lands best when it feels operational, not abstract. These are the kinds of questions Revii should help answer as billing moves into Rev.io.

Billing operations

Which reseller invoices had usage overages last month, and which ones did not produce expected charges?

Finance

Where do Atlas, BRM, and HostPilot totals disagree, and what accounts create the largest reconciliation gap?

Revenue assurance

Show me customers where usage increased but recurring invoice revenue stayed flat or declined.

Product catalog

Which SKUs have active subscribers but no current rate mapping, stale pricing, or missing tax treatment?

Channel billing

Find distributor and reseller accounts where wholesale and retail pricing appear misaligned.

Migration QA

Compare pre-migration BRM charges against Rev.io-generated charges and explain every variance over threshold.

Kuldip, the way I would think about Revii for Intermedia is not as a chatbot sitting on top of billing. It is a billing intelligence layer that helps you modernize safely. You already have a proprietary environment in HostPilot, plus Atlas and BRM carrying a lot of legacy billing logic. Rev.io can become the modern charge generation and rating engine underneath that, while HostPilot continues to serve the customer-facing experience where needed.
