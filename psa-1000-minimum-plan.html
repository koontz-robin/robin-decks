Leadership Proposal

Raise the PSA minimum from $500 to $1,000 without creating sticker shock.

The recommendation is to make $1,000 the new commercial floor for PSA clients, package it as a value-rich Minimum Starter Package, and give each rep a limited monthly supply of ramp promos to bridge qualified smaller clients into the new minimum.

The Decision

Move from a minimum charge to a minimum outcome package.

The current $500 minimum frames PSA as a small-license purchase. The new $1,000 floor should be positioned as the smallest complete operating footprint we are willing to implement and support.

Recommendation

Set the new PSA minimum at $1,000 MRR.

Use the Minimum Starter Package as the default first quote line for new PSA clients and prospects entering proposal stage.

Transition Tool

Use ramp promos, not discounting.

Promos should be scarce, tracked, and time-bound. They are a bridge to the new minimum, not a way to keep selling at $500.

Leadership Ask

Approve floor, promo policy, and operating rules.

Sales can execute if leadership aligns on eligibility, rep allocations, approval workflow, and the non-negotiable step-up to $1,000.

Promo Ramp

Give reps a scarce monthly ramp tool so the minimum increase does not stall qualified deals.

The ramp should be contractual and automatic. A client may start below $1,000 only if the agreement clearly steps them into the $1,000 minimum.

Months 1-2
$500

Launch rate for approved Starter Pack promo deals. This protects early-stage adoption and avoids immediate sticker shock.

Months 3-4
$750

Mid-ramp step. Client is already live, implementation is underway, and the pricing conversation is no longer theoretical.

Month 5+
$1,000

Full Starter Pack minimum begins. No manual renewal, re-approval, or renegotiation required.

Expansion
List

Any users above the included 8 Standard and 5 Field Users are sold at standard list pricing.

Guardrails

Scarcity and rules keep this from becoming a new $500 minimum.

The program needs to feel like rep inventory, not a discount anyone can request after procurement pushes back.

Recommended Allocation

Two ramp promos per rep per month.

  • Unused promos expire at month end.
  • Managers receive a small exception pool for strategic deals.
  • Promos must be logged in Salesforce before proposal is sent.
  • No stacking with other commercial concessions.
Eligibility Rules

Use only where it changes conversion, not margin preference.

  • New PSA logos or qualified cross-sell clients only.
  • Must include the full Starter Pack structure.
  • Must include automatic step-up language in the order form.
  • Cannot be used for clients clearly large enough to start at $1,000.
Approval Path

Rep controlled, manager visible.

  • Rep can use allocated promo without VP approval if rules are met.
  • Manager reviews promo usage weekly.
  • Any third promo in a month requires manager approval.
  • Any ramp longer than four months requires VP approval.
Contract Language

The step-up must be explicit.

  • Quote shows $1,000 Minimum Starter Package as the actual product.
  • Ramp appears as a temporary promotional credit.
  • Credit expires automatically after month four.
  • Client sees the full $1,000 go-forward rate before signature.
Business Impact

The goal is higher floor, cleaner packaging, and fewer under-sized implementations.

The biggest benefit is not only $500 more MRR per client. It is better alignment between what we sell, what we implement, and the minimum team footprint needed for PSA value.

Revenue

Every full-rate client adds $500 MRR over the old floor.

Ten clients sold at the new minimum creates $5,000 more MRR than the same volume under the old floor, before expansion users.

Adoption

The included Field Users reinforce the mobile story.

The Starter Pack nudges clients toward field adoption from day one instead of launching PSA as only a back-office system.

Positioning

Revy AI Outcomes become part of the base value.

Including unlimited AI outcomes makes the $1,000 floor feel tied to modern workflow value rather than a seat-count increase.

Margin Discipline

Ramp promos preserve closeability without hiding the real price.

The client sees $1,000 as the actual package and the ramp as temporary. That is cleaner than discounting the base package.

Sales Behavior

Limited promo inventory forces prioritization.

Reps will spend promos where they believe the deal is real, instead of using discounts as the default negotiation response.

Implementation

Smaller clients still get a complete launch footprint.

The package sets a minimum implementation shape: enough admin, operational, and field users to create visible value.

Rollout Plan

Recommended 30-day launch sequence

Leadership can approve the package quickly if Sales Ops, Finance, and Sales all leave the meeting with clear owners.

Week 1

Approve package and policy.

Finalize $1,000 Starter Pack, included users, ramp credit language, rep allocation, and manager exception pool.

Week 2

Update quoting and order forms.

Create the Minimum Starter Package SKU, temporary promo credit line, Salesforce promo field, and approval tracking.

Week 3

Train managers and reps.

Enable reps with talk track, objection handling, promo rules, examples, and "do not discount the package" guidance.

Week 4

Launch on new proposals.

Apply to new PSA proposals. Review first two weeks of usage in forecast calls and tighten rules if promos are overused.

30 Days

Report back to leadership.

Show proposal volume, promo usage by rep, win rate, average start MRR, full-rate MRR, and exceptions requested.

60 Days

Decide whether to reduce promos.

If conversion holds, reduce promo inventory or shorten ramp to accelerate adoption of the $1,000 floor.
