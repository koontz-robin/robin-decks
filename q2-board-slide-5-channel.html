On Attribution & Referral Tracking
$18,388 is a Floor — Not a Ceiling
The $18,388 YTD reflects the full channel-influenced model — deals where channel played a role through referrals, introductions, co-marketing, and event-sourced leads. These numbers represent a floor.

Referral Tracking
8 referrals formally logged YTD — a known undercount. Lead-source capture at intake has been inconsistent across the sales team. Channel is actively working with Marketing and RevOps to correct this process. Reported referral volume will more accurately reflect actual activity in H2.
⚠️ Tracking Gap — In Progress
The $18,388 reflects what can be formally tracked. The true influence on pipeline, brand consideration, and inbound conversion is materially broader. As attribution tooling and process improve, figures will more accurately reflect channel's full contribution.
Important Context
What Isn't Being Counted
›
When a partner speaks to a prospect about Rev.io and that prospect later visits the website and submits a form, it records as a marketing or inbound lead — not channel.
›
When an SDR reaches an account warmed by a co-marketing post, a podcast Brook appeared on, or a webinar a partner ran — that warmth doesn't show up in an attribution field.
›
LinkedIn co-marketing posts that drive a prospect to research Rev.io independently — recorded as organic/direct, not channel.
›
A partner putting in a strong word that tips a deal already in pipeline — invisible to the current reporting model.
The Real Picture
The $18,388 in channel-influenced MRR reflects what can be formally tracked. The true influence on pipeline, brand consideration, and inbound conversion is materially broader — and largely invisible to the current reporting model. As attribution tooling and process improve, these numbers will more accurately reflect the full scope of channel's contribution.
