Rev.io PSA vs. HaloPSA and Autotask

A side-by-side view for teams evaluating PSA options this month. HaloPSA and Autotask are credible PSA tools, but Rev.io is the better fit when product functionality, onboarding, and the long-term customer experience need to connect service delivery with billing, portal, payments, and revenue visibility.

Prepared July 1, 2026

Why Rev.io Is the Stronger Fit

Choose the PSA that runs the revenue workflow, not just the ticket queue. Rev.io should lead when the prospect wants service desk, projects, time, inventory, customer management, billing, customer portal, payments, analytics, AI automation, and workflow automation working as one business motion.

Rev.io PSA vs. HaloPSA

HaloPSA has a strong PSA story, especially around migration tools, training, and broad MSP adoption. Rev.io stands apart when a PSA needs to connect service work with billing, customer portal, payments, revenue visibility, and operational follow-through.

Quote-to-cash continuity Fewer operational handoffs

Product Functionality

  • Rev.io: Rev.io PSA supports service desk, time tracking, scheduling, customer management, reporting, portal, inventory, projects, AI automation, workflow automation, CommerceHub, and RMM integration.
  • Rev.io: Rev.io PSA works with Rev.io billing and back-office services, helping connect delivered work to invoices, payments, and profitability.
  • HaloPSA: HaloPSA is strong for PSA and service workflow management, but Rev.io provides a broader revenue-operations path when billing and payment flow matter to the decision.

Onboarding Experience

  • Rev.io: Rev.io onboarding is handled in house, with Onboarding Consultants dedicated to the customer's company throughout the implementation.
  • HaloPSA: HaloPSA onboarding often means coordinating implementation resources outside the core vendor team, which can create dependency risk and stretch timelines.
  • Rev.io difference: Rev.io gives the customer a direct onboarding partner who understands their service, billing, projects, workflows, reporting, and customer-facing experience.

Experience as a Customer

  • Rev.io: Teams get a connected operating model for service delivery, customer records, invoice access, payments, analytics, and support workflows.
  • Customer impact: Customers can have a more consistent experience across requests, status updates, invoices, and payments.
  • Decision impact: Rev.io is the stronger fit when the PSA decision needs to improve revenue operations, not only modernize ticketing.

Rev.io PSA vs. Autotask

Autotask is mature and familiar in the MSP market, especially for teams already invested in the Kaseya/Datto ecosystem. Rev.io wins when the buyer wants an easier revenue-operations story, less ecosystem lock-in, and stronger alignment between service, billing, and customer experience.

Connected operations Practical business impact

Product Functionality

  • Rev.io: Rev.io combines PSA functions with billing, portal, payments, analytics, workflow automation, CommerceHub, and RMM integration in the Rev.io operating model.
  • Autotask: Autotask offers service desk, RMM-PSA integration, dashboards, contract/labor/expense invoicing, QuickBooks integration, APIs, and a broad integration ecosystem.
  • Rev.io difference: Rev.io focuses the PSA around fewer commercial handoffs from customer request to delivered work to invoice and payment.

Onboarding Experience

  • Rev.io: Implementation stays with Rev.io's in-house onboarding team, including Onboarding Consultants dedicated to the customer's company from kickoff through go-live.
  • Autotask: Autotask implementations commonly involve outside partner resources or third-party coordination, which can make ownership less consistent and implementation timelines harder to control.
  • Rev.io difference: Rev.io keeps onboarding directly accountable to the service-to-billing flow, executive visibility, and measurable business impact the customer needs.

Experience as a Customer

  • Rev.io: Rev.io can serve as one partner for PSA, billing operations, customer portal, payments, and operational reporting.
  • Autotask: Autotask is a mature PSA within a broader IT platform ecosystem.
  • Decision impact: Rev.io is the clearer choice when the priority is practical business impact instead of expanding the evaluation across every possible IT-stack integration.
