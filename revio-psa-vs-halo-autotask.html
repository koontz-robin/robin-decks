Competitive Battle Cards

Rev.io PSA vs. HaloPSA and Autotask

Prospect-facing decision support for a buyer choosing this month. The sales frame: HaloPSA and Autotask are credible PSA tools, but Rev.io is the better fit when the buyer cares about product functionality tied to billing, a guided implementation path, and a more connected customer experience after go-live.

Prepared July 1, 2026

Recommended Decision Frame

Choose the PSA that runs the revenue workflow, not just the ticket queue. Rev.io should lead when the prospect wants service desk, projects, time, inventory, customer management, billing, customer portal, payments, analytics, AI automation, and workflow automation working as one business motion.

Rev.io PSA vs. HaloPSA

HaloPSA has a strong PSA story, especially around migration tools, training, and broad MSP adoption. Rev.io wins the conversation when the buyer needs a PSA that is tightly connected to billing, customer portal, payments, revenue visibility, and operational follow-through.

Rev.io angle: quote-to-cash Decision urgency: reduce handoffs

Product Functionality

  • Rev.io advantage: Rev.io PSA is positioned across service desk, time tracking, scheduling, customer management, reporting, portal, inventory, projects, AI automation, workflow automation, CommerceHub, and RMM integration.
  • Rev.io billing edge: Rev.io PSA works with Rev.io billing and back-office services, which matters if the prospect wants service delivery connected to invoices, payments, and profitability.
  • Halo watchout: Halo can look strong in a service-desk-heavy checklist, so keep the conversation focused on what happens after the ticket: agreement accuracy, billing readiness, customer portal, and cash collection.

Onboarding Experience

  • Rev.io position: Use a guided business-process implementation, not a generic software install. Anchor onboarding around the prospect's service, billing, project, and customer workflows.
  • Halo reality: Halo publicly describes discovery, design, build, UAT, training, go-live, hypercare, and project review. That is credible, so do not dismiss it.
  • Rev.io counter: Rev.io's advantage is alignment: implementation should connect PSA configuration to billing operations and executive revenue outcomes from day one.

Experience as a Customer

  • Rev.io story: Customers get a single operating model for service delivery, customer records, invoice access, payments, analytics, and support workflows.
  • Buyer value: Fewer swivel-chair moments for teams and fewer disconnected experiences for customers submitting tickets, checking status, receiving invoices, and paying.
  • Close this month: Rev.io is the lower-regret choice if the prospect wants the PSA decision to improve revenue operations immediately, not just modernize ticketing.
Talk track: "Halo is a good PSA, but the question is whether you want a PSA that mainly organizes service work, or one that connects service work to billing, portal, payments, and revenue visibility. If this decision is happening this month, Rev.io gives you the cleaner path to business impact."

Rev.io PSA vs. Autotask

Autotask is mature and familiar in the MSP market, especially for teams already invested in the Kaseya/Datto ecosystem. Rev.io wins when the buyer wants an easier revenue-operations story, less ecosystem lock-in, and stronger alignment between service, billing, and customer experience.

Rev.io angle: connected operations Decision urgency: avoid overbuying complexity

Product Functionality

  • Rev.io advantage: Rev.io combines PSA functions with billing, portal, payments, analytics, workflow automation, CommerceHub, and RMM integration in the Rev.io operating model.
  • Autotask reality: Autotask promotes service desk, RMM-PSA integration, dashboards, contract/labor/expense invoicing, QuickBooks integration, APIs, and 170+ integrations.
  • Rev.io counter: Integration count is not the same as workflow simplicity. Rev.io's strongest message is fewer commercial handoffs from customer request to delivered work to invoice and payment.

Onboarding Experience

  • Rev.io position: Keep implementation tied to how the prospect sells, delivers, bills, reports, and collects, rather than only configuring PSA modules.
  • Autotask reality: Datto says implementation schedules vary by team needs, skills, and availability, and offers resources, documentation, learning, and support.
  • Rev.io counter: For a prospect deciding this month, Rev.io can keep onboarding centered on the business outcomes they need now: cleaner service-to-billing flow and executive visibility.

Experience as a Customer

  • Rev.io story: Rev.io should feel like one partner for PSA, billing operations, customer portal, payments, and operational reporting.
  • Autotask watchout: A broad platform suite can be powerful, but buyers may inherit more ecosystem decisions than they need for the specific business problem.
  • Close this month: Rev.io is the clearer choice if the prospect wants practical business impact without making the evaluation about every possible IT-stack integration.
Talk track: "Autotask is a mature PSA, but maturity can also mean inheriting an ecosystem. Rev.io is the better fit if your priority is connecting service delivery, billing, portal, payments, and revenue reporting into one practical operating model."

Quick Comparison Points

Product Functionality

Lead with Rev.io's breadth: service desk, time, scheduling, CRM, reporting, portal, inventory, projects, AI/workflow automation, CommerceHub, RMM, billing, and payments.

Onboarding

Position Rev.io onboarding around the prospect's real operating model: how they quote, deliver, bill, collect, and report, not just how they configure tickets.

Customer Experience

Rev.io gives the prospect a cleaner story for employees and end customers because service, portal, invoices, and payments can stay connected.

Discovery Questions That Favor Rev.io

When a ticket or project is completed, how much manual work is required before the customer is billed accurately?
Do customers have one place to see service updates, invoices, and payments, or do they move across separate systems?
Which reports does leadership trust today: service delivery reports, billing reports, or spreadsheets built after the fact?
How important is reducing billing leakage compared with simply improving ticket management?
If you go live this month, what workflow needs to be right first: tickets, projects, billing, payments, or all of them together?
Do you want the PSA to fit your revenue process, or are you comfortable adapting revenue operations around a generic PSA workflow?
