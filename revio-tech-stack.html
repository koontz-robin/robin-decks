Stack Roles
02 / 05

Each platform has a clear job in the revenue engine.

The strongest version of the stack is not more tools. It is a clean division of labor where every system owns a distinct part of the motion.

HubSpot

Marketing

Captures and nurtures demand, manages marketing audiences, and supports campaign-to-lead visibility.

Salesforce

CRM

The system of record for accounts, contacts, opportunities, pipeline, ownership, activity, and leadership reporting.

Outreach

Engagement / Dialer

Turns target accounts and contacts into sequenced rep activity across email, phone, tasks, and follow-up workflows.

ZoomInfo

TAM Expansion

Expands account and contact coverage so the team can identify new territories, contacts, buying committees, and whitespace.

OpenClaw

Sales Team Bots

Automates sales team operating work: dashboards, alerts, routing, follow-ups, research packets, and rep-facing assistants.

Clay

Enrichment

Combines external data sources and AI research to turn raw accounts into usable targeting and personalization fields.

Claude

Document Design

Converts sales strategy and source material into polished documents, decks, narratives, business cases, and customer-ready assets.

Rox

AI Automated Prospecting

Runs signal-based AI prospecting to identify, research, prioritize, and engage accounts with less manual prep from reps.

DocuSign

Esignature

Moves approved commercial terms through signature quickly and gives sales a clean handoff from verbal yes to executed agreement.

Notion

Knowledgebase / Meeting Transcription

Stores reusable sales knowledge, meeting notes, transcripts, operating context, and internal reference material.

Revenue Workflow
04 / 05

From market map to signed agreement.

This is the stack as a workflow, not a vendor list: identify the market, enrich the account, automate prospecting, execute engagement, then package and close.

Step 01

Build the market

ZoomInfo expands TAM coverage and identifies accounts, contacts, buying committees, and whitespace.

Step 02

Enrich and segment

Clay turns account lists into enriched targeting fields, signals, and personalization inputs.

Step 03

Automate prospecting

Rox and OpenClaw create leverage: automated prospecting, sales team bots, reporting, routing, and alerts.

Step 04

Engage and convert

HubSpot captures demand, Outreach drives rep engagement, and Salesforce records the funnel.

Step 05

Document and close

Notion captures the meeting record, Claude packages the story, and DocuSign moves approved terms through signature.

Leadership Message
05 / 05

The strategic shift: reps spend less time assembling context and more time converting it.

The stack should reduce manual work, improve targeting quality, make activity more measurable, and shorten the path from signal to signed agreement.

Operating principles

Salesforce owns truthAll account, contact, opportunity, and pipeline reporting should reconcile back to CRM.
Knowledge stays reusableNotion captures meeting transcripts, notes, and internal context so learning compounds across the team.
AI creates leverageRox, OpenClaw, Clay, and Claude remove prep work, research burden, and manual document production.
Engagement stays measurableOutreach and HubSpot should make activity, campaign impact, and conversion visible.
Execution closes the loopDocuSign turns approved commercial intent into signed agreements with less delay.
