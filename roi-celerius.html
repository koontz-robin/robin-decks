Section 1

What We Heard

Over four discovery calls in April 2026, Woody and Tim walked us through exactly how Celerius operates today. You're a 9-person MSP north of Baltimore on Autotask — migrated just eight months ago — and already identifying the gaps. These are your words, not ours.

License Syncing Is Your Biggest Time Drain

Syncing vendor licenses — Sophos, Cove, Microsoft M365 via TD SYNNEX, Duo, and others — is done entirely manually. Every time a customer adds or removes a license, someone has to catch it, reconcile it, and make sure billing reflects it. No automation, no alerts, no safety net.

"License syncing is the most time-consuming thing." — Tim

Kaseya Quote Manager Creates Constant Double Entry

Every quote built in Kaseya Quote Manager has to be manually re-entered into Autotask to become a ticket and an agreement. There's no automation between the two — meaning every sale generates duplicate work before service delivery even starts.

"Manual entry required for projects and tickets due to the separate quoting platform."

Payroll Requires a Manual Report Process

Tim tracks payroll by pulling ticket time entries from Autotask and building a manual report every pay period. Autotask doesn't separate payroll time from billable time, so the whole process requires customization and reconciliation. Celerius was actively considering paying ADP separately for timekeeping to solve this.

"We'd been looking at using ADP for timekeeping. This does make it easier." — Woody

Updating Agreement Pricing Is a Nightmare

When pricing needs to change — even a 3% annual increase — someone has to go into every single agreement individually to update it. With hundreds of active contracts, this is a massive time sink that discourages pricing discipline and costs real money in margin erosion.

"Global changes to agreements and prices — that's one of the bigger things for us." — Woody

Maryland Tax Complexity Is Handled Manually

Maryland has three separate tax rates — sales tax, digital tax, and IT tech tax — each applying differently depending on the service type. The current setup requires manual tax code assignment and oversight, creating compliance risk and billing errors.

"We have sales tax, digital tax, and IT tech tax. Based on what it is, it gets taxed differently." — Woody

Co-Managed IT Pricing Is a Roadblock

Celerius has 5-6 in-house IT people at client locations who need to create and work tickets. Autotask's co-manage pricing equals a full technician license — $25+ more per user than what was originally quoted — making the feature economically unviable to deploy.

"The price for co-manage is the same as a full technician license. That doesn't work." — Woody
