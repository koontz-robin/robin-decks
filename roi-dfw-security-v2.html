Background

Understanding the DFW Security Structure

DFW Security operates as two related entities — each with distinct billing and payment needs. Rev.io is positioned to unify both.

MGM LP — RMR Billing Entity

The entity that bills recurring monthly revenue from alarm monitoring contracts. Mark Follis and Marty each own over 25%. This is the entity where payment processing volume is highest — monthly ACH and card charges flowing from residential and commercial monitoring customers.

MGM Security Services / DFW Security — Sales & Installation Entity

The sales and installation arm. No individual owns more than 25%. This entity handles field work, equipment procurement, job tickets, and customer-facing service operations — the core PSA workflow.

Section 1

What We Heard During the Sales Process

Pain points raised directly by DFW Security's leadership during pre-sale discovery conversations and email correspondence with the Rev.io team. These reflect strategic priorities the business identified before any platform engagement.

ℹ️ This version of the deck draws exclusively from sales discovery calls and email correspondence — not from any post-sale or onboarding sessions.

Payment Processor Penalizes Volume

When DFW Security shared their full ACH transaction volume with their current processor to negotiate a better rate, the processor responded by raising the price. High-volume RMR businesses should benefit from scale — not get penalized for it. This was the sharpest pain point raised during the Payments sales conversation.

"I gave you 100X the volume of ACH's and you raised the price 20%. Help me understand that."
— Mark Follis

Legacy System Data Migration Risk

DFW Security is migrating records from two platforms: Sedona (security-specific) and a legacy CRM. This dual-stream import was a known concern entering the relationship — clean data and continuity of billing and service history were explicit requirements raised during the sales process.

"We would need to bring records in from our current CRM."
— Tyler Morton, Project Manager

Dual-Entity Billing Complexity

Recurring billing flows through MGM LP while field service and installation runs through MGM Security Services / DFW Security. Managing two entities across a fragmented payment stack creates reconciliation overhead and creates risk of billing errors across the RMR book.

No Unified Visibility Across Service + Billing

With PSA and payment processing in separate systems, Guy and the leadership team lack a single view of customer status — open tickets, billing state, and payment history require jumping between platforms. This was a key driver for the PSA + Payments bundled opportunity.
