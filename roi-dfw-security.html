Background

Understanding the DFW Security Structure

DFW Security operates as two related entities — each with distinct billing and payment needs. Rev.io is positioned to unify both.

MGM LP — RMR Billing Entity

The entity that bills recurring monthly revenue from alarm monitoring contracts. Mark Follis and Marty each own over 25%. This is the entity where payment processing volume is highest — monthly ACH and card charges flowing from residential and commercial monitoring customers.

MGM Security Services / DFW Security — Sales & Installation Entity

The sales and installation arm. No individual owns more than 25%. This entity handles field work, equipment procurement, job tickets, and customer-facing service operations — the core PSA workflow. Already onboarding to Rev.io PSA as of April 2026.

Section 1

What We Heard

Based on discovery conversations and PSA onboarding sessions, here are the operational friction points DFW Security is navigating daily. These are Guy Page's and Tyler Morton's words — we've simply organized and quantified them.

Payment Processor Penalizes Volume

When DFW Security provided their full ACH transaction volume to the current processor, the rate went UP 20%. High-volume RMR businesses should benefit from scale — not get penalized for it.

"I gave you 100X the volume of ACH's and you raised the price 20%. Help me understand that."
— Mark Follis

No SKUs — Vendor Ordering Is Broken

The current system lacks proper SKU/part number support, making it difficult to order equipment from vendors accurately. Every PO requires manual entry and notation of each line item — a recurring drag on Tyler and the ops team.

"No SKUs so I don't know how we're going to order equipment properly from vendors."
— Guy Page, CEO

Ticket Scheduling Blind Spots

On the ticket level, technicians and dispatchers can't see when or who a job is scheduled for. To find out if Mrs. Jones is on tomorrow's calendar, you have to drill into the calendar and search day by day — wasted dispatch time at scale.

"You have to pull up the calendar and search day by day to see when Mrs. Jones is scheduled."
— Guy Page, CEO

Dual-Platform Data Migration

DFW Security is migrating records from two systems: Sedona (security-specific platform) and an additional CRM/management tool. Clean data import is critical — fragmented history means billing and service continuity risk during transition.

"We would need to bring records in from our current CRM."
— Tyler Morton, Product Manager

PO Entry Is Too Cumbersome

Having to individually enter and annotate each item on a purchase order is unsustainable at their operation size. Specific vendor verbiage requirements add another manual layer before POs can even be submitted.

"Having to individually enter and notate each item on a PO is way too cumbersome."
— Guy Page, CEO

Mid-Day Software Instability

Bug fixes being pushed during business hours have disrupted live onboarding sessions and daily operations. For a field service operation running dispatched technicians, unexpected software breaks mid-shift carry real cost and customer risk.

"She mentioned how that happens regularly — we hit a new bug that must have been pushed in the middle of the day."
— Guy Page, CEO
