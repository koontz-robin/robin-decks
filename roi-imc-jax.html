Current State

The biggest issue is not one missing feature. It is fragmented customer context.

IMC Jax is managing a telecom and service business without a dedicated PSA. Customer information is split across multiple records, folders, and manual processes, which makes it harder for sales, service, field, and finance teams to see the full customer picture.

Multiple customer records

Different services for the same customer can live as separate records, which prevents a one-click view of every active service, location, agreement, and request.

Manual recurring agreements

Recurring services and renewals are currently managed through email and shared files instead of structured agreements with terms, services, and reminders.

No formal ticket intake

Trouble tickets, outside-team assignments, and sold work orders need a controlled process that can move completed work back toward billing.

Limited field access

Field teams need mobile access to ticket details, assignments, updates, and job context without relying on office staff to relay information.

Billing decision pending

The team is evaluating how SkySwitch billing, direct Rev.io billing, and Rev.io PSA should fit together as the operating model matures.

Implementation clarity matters

IMC Jax wants a clear view of migration scope, timing, training, limitations, and support before committing to the transition.

Platform credibility context: Rev.io's core product is a telecom billing engine with roughly 20 years in market. The PSA, originally Tigerpaw, was acquired about 3 years ago and rebuilt from the ground up as an all-in-one back-office system covering CRM, quoting/e-signature, ticketing, inventory, and billing without separate paid modules.

Billing Decision

The SkySwitch vs. direct Rev.io billing question needs a clean comparison.

IMC Jax is already familiar with Rev.io through SkySwitch, but the SkySwitch environment is different from a direct Rev.io billing relationship. The best customer-facing explanation should be simple: PSA can help run the service business, while the billing path determines how much control IMC Jax has over product catalog, billing rules, customer portal, reporting, API access, telecom usage, and tax.

Sharpest billing point: If IMC Jax stays on SkySwitch billing, that billing instance has zero integration with Rev.io PSA. The decision is not just about more or less control; it determines whether billing can participate in the same operating workflow as PSA.

Stay on SkySwitch billing

Lower change management, but the SkySwitch billing instance will not integrate with Rev.io PSA. Billing and PSA remain separate operating systems.

Use PSA billing for non-telecom

Good fit for circuits, cameras, service tickets, projects, work orders, and non-usage-based items that do not require telecom tax/rating.

Move to direct Rev.io billing

More control over billing configuration, catalog, reporting, portal, API access, and future operating-model flexibility, with separate pricing and migration planning.

Why billing and PSA are not fully unified today: usage rating/CDR ingestion and telecom taxation remain the key technical blockers. That context matters because it supports a straight, credible discussion about what can be unified now, what requires direct billing, and what should be staged over time.
Business Case

Where the value should come from

The ROI case should focus on operational leverage, not just software replacement. The strongest value pools are reduced administrative effort, faster access to customer context, fewer missed billing handoffs, cleaner renewal management, and better visibility into customer profitability.

Fewer information searches

Reduce the time spent finding customer data, service details, documents, ticket history, and current account status across folders and email.

Cleaner billing handoffs

Completed work orders and service changes can flow toward billing with less reliance on memory, side conversations, or manual re-entry.

Renewal discipline

Agreement terms and reminders can create a proactive customer-engagement rhythm rather than depending on manual follow-up.

Better team visibility

Inside staff, outside field teams, sales, and leadership can work from the same customer context instead of maintaining separate views of the customer.

Scalable customer hierarchy

Parent-child and related-account structures support multi-location customers and more complex relationships without flattening everything into one list.

More informed growth

Profitability reporting and cleaner exports give leadership a better view of where service work, recurring revenue, and customer effort are creating or eroding margin.

Migration risk to manage: The July 20 demo surfaced that IMC Jax's legacy QuickBooks and back-office data need cleanup before import. The 5-6 week implementation timeline is still realistic only if the data export arrives early enough for Rev.io to assess migration complexity and data quality.
