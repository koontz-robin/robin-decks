Section 1

What We Heard

Security integrators running mixed residential/commercial work with both project installs and recurring monitoring revenue consistently hit the same operational walls. These are the challenges we hear from operators at ICS's stage of growth.

Fragmented Quoting & Job Management

Multi-system installs — cameras, access control, fire, data cabling — require detailed project scopes. When quoting, job creation, and invoicing live in separate tools (or spreadsheets), every project involves manual handoffs that slow delivery and invite billing errors.

Every job that starts as a quote requires someone to manually re-enter it as a ticket, then again as an invoice.

Recurring Monitoring Billing Is Manual

Monitoring contracts are your most predictable revenue stream — but without automated recurring billing, they require ongoing manual oversight every month. Missed charges, rate changes, and cancelled customers are hard to catch before they cost you real money.

One missed monitoring contract per month is real revenue that's already been earned — just not collected.

Field Technician Dispatch Lacks Visibility

Coordinating techs across residential and commercial sites on Long Island — across alarm, camera, fire, and IT work — requires real-time visibility into who's available and where. Without a connected dispatch board, scheduling becomes reactive and jobs slip.

Dispatch by text and phone call is how you manage five techs, not fifteen.

IT Services Revenue Hard to Track

ICS offers a full suite of IT services alongside physical security — a strong differentiator, but one that adds billing complexity. Managed IT services often require a different billing model (per-seat, per-device, per-service) than security installs, and mixing them manually is an operational headache.

Physical security billing and IT managed services billing have different models — trying to manage both in the same spreadsheet breaks down fast.

No Unified View of Jobs, Revenue & Customer History

With 3,000+ customers and multiple service lines, leadership needs visibility into what's installed at each site, what's under contract, and what's outstanding — without pulling from three different systems. Customer context should travel with the job, not live in someone's head.

When a tech shows up on a service call, they should already know what's at the site — not have to call the office to find out.

Growth Strains Manual Processes

35 years of growth built on relationships and custom solutions is a competitive advantage — but manual billing, ticketing, and scheduling don't scale the same way great service does. The goal is to keep the personalized experience customers love while automating the back-office work that slows you down.

Adding 100 customers shouldn't require adding two admin headcount to keep up.
