Section 1

What We Heard

Intermedia is running three separate billing systems — two of which are end-of-life — and the manual overhead of keeping them in sync is costing real developer time and creating operational risk. These aren't hypothetical problems. They're your team's daily reality.

Three Billing Systems, Zero Integration

Atlas (legacy provisioning/billing — acquired), BRM (Oracle Billing & Revenue Management — UCaaS/CCaaS), and HostPilot (invoicing, payments, customer portal) must all stay in sync. Every transaction passes through multiple systems before it reaches a customer.

"Convoluted billing process — transactions pass through and reconcile three separate systems, two inherited and legacy."

Developer Time Burned on Legacy Maintenance

Atlas and BRM are effectively end-of-life. Developers are actively maintaining two systems that Intermedia wants to decommission — time that should go toward product and growth, not keeping legacy software alive.

"Rev.io will eliminate the need to maintain three separate billing platforms and save developer time on end-of-life maintenance."

Complex Multi-Tier Channel Billing

Intermedia bills direct customers (A/A1), reseller partners (D), and distributors (B) across a three-tier pricing structure — distributor pays $9, sells to reseller at $13, reseller bills customer at $16. Each tier needs its own invoice format (wholesale vs. retail pricing on a single EOC).

"Biggest gap is representing the hierarchy on invoices — wholesale vs. retail pricing breakout that partners need to see."

CDR Rating & Usage Complexity

PSTN calls generate billable CDRs; OnNet calls do not. Intermedia needs an FTP-based CDR loading process, usage allowances with overage billing, and potentially two separate rating environments — one for wholesale distributor billing, one for retail end-customer billing.

"Set up FTP site where Intermedia dumps CDR files for Rev.io processing."

~1,000 SKU Product Catalog

Product catalog complexity requires approximately 1,000 SKUs to represent different representations of how they bill each service — with products needing separate currency handling, billing time elements, and quick launch/update capability via CPQ.

"Need ~1,000 SKUs for different representations. CPQ: quickly launch new SKUs, price point updates, pricing modifications."

AI & Reporting as Strategic Priority

Leadership has identified AI as a major initiative. The current fragmented systems cannot deliver the revenue leakage detection, LLM-based reporting, or analytics capabilities that Intermedia's leadership is asking for. A modern billing platform is a prerequisite.

"Leadership indicated AI as major initiative — revenue leakage detection, LLM/reporting capabilities. Implementation likely needs PSA rather than legacy billing."
Section 2

Current Architecture vs. Proposed State

Today, Intermedia operates a three-system billing architecture where Atlas and BRM feed HostPilot for invoice generation and payments. Rev.io replaces the charge generation and CDR processing layer entirely — with HostPilot remaining as the customer-facing invoice and payment surface.

Today — Three Systems, Manual Reconciliation
Atlas — Legacy provisioning + billing (acquired, end-of-life). Hardware provisioning, legacy VoIP/SIPT billing, invoice generation for legacy products.
BRM (Oracle) — UCaaS/CCaaS billing, quote/tax integration, usage buckets, channel pricing. Feeds to and from Atlas. End-of-life maintenance burden.
HostPilot — Intermedia's in-house platform. Manages invoices, payments, customer portal, and BCA/data services billing. Stays in place.
Manual syncing between all three systems — semi-automated at best, prone to reconciliation errors and developer overhead.
→
With Rev.io — Simplified, Single Billing Engine
Rev.io Billing — Replaces Atlas + BRM as the single charge generation and CDR processing engine. Handles all usage rating, channel pricing, recurring billing, and invoice data generation.
CDR Processing — FTP-based CDR intake from Intermedia's SBC/upstream providers. PSTN calls rated, OnNet excluded. Usage allowances + overage billing automated.
HostPilot (remains) — Consumes invoice XML from Rev.io. Invoices customers and processes payments. Payments flow back to Rev.io via API for balance reconciliation.
Avalara tax integration — Rev.io continues feeding Intermedia's existing Avalara setup for telecom tax compliance.
Atlas (provisioning only) — May remain as provisioning system if needed, decoupled from billing logic.
