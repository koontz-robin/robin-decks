Executive Summary

The goal is a cleaner operating model from quote to service delivery.

LAE Solutions has a strong operating foundation, but day-to-day work still crosses multiple systems for CRM, quoting, field service, project execution, billing, monitoring, and reporting. Rev.io PSA Web gives LAE a path to connect those workflows around a single operational record while preserving the project billing, field execution, and accounting discipline the business already depends on.

Current State

LAE's workflow is effective, but too much work happens twice.

The current stack uses separate systems for CRM, quoting, field-service execution, and accounting. That has helped the business keep moving, but it creates repeated handoffs, manual reconciliation, and uncertainty about which platform should be the long-term system of record.

CRM and quoting are split

Customer and opportunity activity begins outside the field-service platform, while accepted proposals have to become operational work somewhere else.

Billing is recreated manually

The initial deposit invoice is generated from the quoting flow, but later project invoices are rebuilt manually in the field-service system and reconciled in accounting.

Project billing is phased

Common project structures include deposit, progress, and final-payment models. Any replacement workflow must support that rhythm without slowing cash collection.

Inventory visibility needs attention

Inventory and truck-stock workflows have been a gap in alternate paths under consideration and should be validated directly by warehouse and operations users.

Sales follow-up can leak

Tasks, follow-ups, and handoffs can fall through cracks when CRM, quoting, and operations do not share a clean workflow.

Monitoring workflows are changing

LAE is moving its central station monitoring platform from UCC to CMS, creating a timely reason to validate how Rev.io can support alarm-monitoring account data.

Recommended Workflow

Show the complete path from proposal to operational work.

The strongest proof point is a practical workflow, not a generic feature tour. LAE should see one accepted proposal become a Rev.io quote, move through approval, create a job or project, support phased invoicing, feed field execution, and reconcile cleanly with accounting.

Current workflow pressure

Customer, proposal, project, billing, field, and monitoring activity can live in different places, which creates handoffs and extra review before the work is fully visible.

Rev.io target workflow

Rev.io can become the operational hub for customer records, quotes, jobs, projects, tickets, time, invoices, field updates, monitoring account data, assets, and reporting.

Portal Integration / Workflow Scope

Define exactly what the Portal-to-Rev.io workflow should cover.

LAE should evaluate the Portal integration as a controlled handoff from customer-facing proposal activity into Rev.io's operating workflow. The goal is not only to move quote data; it is to make clear which records are created, which teams review them, and where downstream project, service, billing, and reporting work begins.

1. Customer and contact alignmentKeep Portal and Rev.io customer/contact records synchronized so accepted proposal activity lands on the right operational account.
2. Proposal-to-quote syncMove accepted Portal proposal line items into a Rev.io quote, including product catalog creation when an item does not already exist.
3. Internal review gateUse the preferred Portal status trigger to let LAE review the quote before downstream job, project, ticket, or billing activity is created.
4. Operational work creationConvert approved quote activity into the right Rev.io work path: project for installs, ticket for service, and recurring account data where monitoring applies.
5. Billing and finance handoffPreserve deposit, progress, final, and service invoice logic while keeping finance clear on what remains in QuickBooks versus Rev.io.
6. Reporting visibilityExpose the full customer path from proposal through field work, invoicing, monitoring context, and management reporting.

Evaluation Criteria

The evaluation should answer the questions that matter to LAE.

1. Does the Portal flow actually work?

LAE should see a real accepted proposal move into Rev.io and understand exactly what syncs, what does not, and which status triggers the handoff.

2. Is invoicing better, not lateral?

The new workflow must simplify deposit, progress, final, and service billing rather than forcing the team to recreate invoices in a different place.

3. Can each department validate fit?

Billing, warehouse, operations, and field teams need short role-specific demos instead of one long generalized walkthrough.

4. Does Rev.io simplify enough of the stack?

Rev.io should be evaluated against the full operating workflow, not only as a replacement for one current tool.

5. Is the roadmap acceptable?

Open items such as notes, signed PDFs, invoice data, leads, and Zoho integration should be acknowledged and mapped to decision risk.

6. Is this a five-year foundation?

The platform should support LAE's strategic shift toward larger commercial work, recurring inspections, and more disciplined operating visibility.

Bottom Line

Rev.io is the right fit if the workflow proves itself end to end.

The remaining work is to prove the Portal integration end to end, confirm the finance-ready invoicing path, show role-specific workflows, and be transparent about roadmap items such as CMS, Zoho, leads, notes, signed PDFs, and invoice-level Portal data.

How to read this business case: LAE should evaluate whether Rev.io gives the team a clear operating layer connecting sales, quoting, projects, service, field labor, billing, monitoring, and reporting in a way each department can trust.
