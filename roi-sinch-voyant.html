Section 1

The Problems We're Solving

Voyant chose to evaluate Rev.io because CustomCall (CCDS) stopped being a platform you could trust to grow with. These are the business reasons that brought us to the table.

CCDS Has Lost Voyant's Confidence

CustomCall's CCDS platform has been deprioritized by its own organization. Voyant's assessment is that it is no longer a scalable, reliable foundation for your billing operations. Continuing to depend on a platform that the vendor has effectively sidelined creates operational and strategic risk that compounds over time.

"Sinch is looking at migrating away from CCDS. It's been deprioritized and lost their confidence as a scalable platform."

Reseller Billing Scalability Constraint

Voyant's growth strategy depends on enabling resellers to bill their own customers through a bill-on-behalf model. Today, reseller billing flows through Smartbox — a workaround, not a scalable architecture. As the reseller base grows, the current structure limits how fast and efficiently Voyant can onboard and support new reseller partners.

"Bring Voyant a scalable platform that allows them to build Bill-On-Behalf instances off of for their resellers."

Developer Resources Tied to Legacy Maintenance

Maintaining and integrating with CCDS requires ongoing developer time. Every hour spent keeping a legacy system running is an hour not spent on Voyant's product, growth initiatives, or customer experience. The longer CCDS stays in place, the more developer capital it consumes.

"Reviewing requirements documentation to ensure no glaring functionality gaps when comparing Rev.io to CustomCall."

Pricing Volatility Risk

CCDS's volume-based pricing model creates monthly billing fluctuations as Voyant's subscriber base grows. Rev.io's flat-rate annual revenue band model provides predictable, budgetable costs — a significant advantage for financial planning and forecasting at Voyant's scale.

"Instead of billing based on monthly billing volume, we're positioning a flat rate within annual revenue bands — helps budget costs more accurately." — Ryan Koontz

Implementation Capacity Risk of Delay

The implementation was already deferred once — from Q3 2025 to January 2026 — due to team capacity concerns. Every month of continued delay is another month Voyant's billing operations run on a platform that has already lost internal confidence. The cost of delay is real and grows over time.

"Technical and/or Operations team pushing back on starting migration in 2025. Dave agreed to hold kick-off until January if contract could be finalized."

Reseller Billing Feed from Smartbox

RK specifically identified the need to replicate how reseller billing information flows from Smartbox into the new platform. This is a defined, documentable workflow that Rev.io is equipped to support — ensuring no disruption to existing reseller billing relationships during migration.

"RK interested in discussing how Voyant could feed reseller billing information into Rev.io similar to how it works today with Smartbox." — Ryan Koontz
