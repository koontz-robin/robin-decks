What We Know

SouthEast is not just buying billing software.

The full deal record shows a telecom operator trying to modernize the operating core: recurring billing, telecom tax, AR payments, customer portal access, service records, dispatch, inventory, and historical CRM/ticketing data. Rev.io should be positioned as a staged operating model: Billing/Payments/Portal first if needed, PSA in tandem or as a second phase once field-service fit is proven.

Recurring endpoint billing

Pool phones, elevator lines, fire/life-safety communications, HVAC internet, camera systems, access control, POTS replacement, and internet services create monthly recurring charges plus equipment, trip, labor, install, and promotional-rate scenarios.

QuickBooks is the pressure point

QuickBooks Online handles finance today, but telecom tax is manually lumped and cross-checked in Excel, recurring and one-time invoices are split, collections are manual, and the prior desktop-to-online migration is still being reconciled.

Tax risk is the sharpest wedge

The new 16-state self-install pool phone motion creates municipality-level telecom tax exposure in some states. This is not just a time-savings problem; QuickBooks Online structurally cannot handle the full tax requirement.

Payments have real upside

QuickBooks Payments is card-only for them, large customers route through PayModeX/Avid Exchange, and SouthEast is absorbing roughly $15K-$16K/year in processing fees. Rev.io can support integrated AR, ACH, portal payment methods, and payment reporting.

Portal solves a known customer problem

The Enterprise Portal demo answered the multi-account/same-email concern with parent-child or single-login configuration, bill-center users, invoice downloads, payment methods, invoice-specific payments, and selectively shared reports.

PSA must prove service fit

Ranger MSP is outdated but holds historical CRM/ticketing data. The PSA demo needs to prove CRM, time tracking, scheduling/dispatch, inventory, mobile GPS, ticketing, and service workflow fit before SouthEast commits to replacing that part of the stack.
