What We Know

SouthEast is not just buying billing software.

The public business model points to recurring, compliance-sensitive services: emergency pool phones, elevator phones, POTS replacement, fire alarm panel communication, internet, static IPs, and support. The Salesforce demo notes show they are looking for a platform path that can begin with QuickBooks Online and expand into PSA when RangerMSP data is ready.

Recurring endpoint billing

Pool phones, elevator phones, fire panel communication, wireless POTS replacement, and internet services create monthly recurring charges plus equipment, trip, labor, and install fees.

Customer hierarchy matters

The team asked about one customer portal login across multiple customer accounts via hierarchy, which is highly relevant for property managers, HOAs, builders, and multi-site accounts.

Finance wants turnkey coverage

Josh specifically asked for more AP detail because the goal is to process AR and AP through Rev.io for complete bookkeeping and reporting.

Integration path must be staged

They asked whether Rev.io can start with QuickBooks Online migration and bring RangerMSP into Rev.io PSA later. That suggests a phased implementation will land better than a big-bang pitch.

Tax/service location accuracy

Demo recap covered service-location addresses, automated tax calculation, and compliance reporting. For life-safety communications, address accuracy is not cosmetic.

Active buying motion

Salesforce shows a Billing opportunity at Stage 3 and a PSA opportunity at Stage 2, both with July 31 close dates. Next meeting is positioned around portal, pricing, migration, onboarding, and AP.

What changes operationally

Current-state risk

QuickBooks remains the finance anchor, RangerMSP stores internal customer records, customer account hierarchy needs a portal answer, AP is not yet clearly mapped, and field/service activity can require manual billing handoff. That creates friction exactly where SouthEast wants a turnkey bookkeeping and reporting workflow.

Rev.io target state

Billing rules, service addresses, customer portal, payments, collections, tax calculation, quote/order workflow, and service activity live in a connected model. QuickBooks can be handled first, while PSA/RangerMSP migration becomes a second phase instead of a blocker.
