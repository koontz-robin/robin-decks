Executive Summary

The ROI is in fewer systems, cleaner handoffs, and faster cash visibility.

SouthEast Telephone sells life-safety communications where recurring service, accurate service addresses, emergency endpoint records, customer portal access, taxes, payments, and support workflows all matter. The business case is not simply replacing software; it is creating a staged operating model that starts with Billing, Payments, Enterprise Portal, tax, and QuickBooks Online integration, then adds PSA once the team validates field-service fit.

What We Know

SouthEast is not just buying billing software.

The public business model points to recurring, compliance-sensitive services: emergency pool phones, elevator phones, POTS replacement, fire alarm panel communication, internet, static IPs, and support. The right path can begin with billing, payments, tax, customer portal, and finance workflows, then expand into PSA and field-service operations when the team is ready.

Recurring endpoint billing

Pool phones, elevator phones, fire panel communication, wireless POTS replacement, and internet services create monthly recurring charges plus equipment, trip, labor, and install fees.

Customer hierarchy matters

The Enterprise Portal demo showed parent-child account structure, bill-center users, multiple payment methods, invoice-specific payments, downloadable invoices, customer-visible reports, and single-login access across multiple accounts.

Finance wants cleaner coverage

The desired outcome is a cleaner AR, payment, reporting, and reconciliation workflow that reduces manual bookkeeping effort and improves visibility.

Implementation path should be staged

Rev.io can support a phased approach: start with billing, payments, portal, tax, and finance workflows, then add PSA in tandem or as a second phase depending on adoption readiness.

Tax/service location accuracy

They bill across 16 states, with some telecom taxes calculated down to the municipality level. QuickBooks Online cannot solve that structurally; today it requires manual tax lumping and Excel cross-checks.

Open implementation questions

Remaining questions center on usage-data formats, Enterprise Portal value, Payments statements, Ranger MSP data access, QuickBooks Online reconciliation timing, and adoption across finance, field, and service teams.

7/8 Demo Recap

The latest demo expanded the business case beyond an intro follow-up.

The 7/8 meeting covered CDR/usage ingestion, customer account structure, quote-to-cash workflow, tax automation, product catalog and promotional pricing, and data migration/onboarding. 7/10 Meeting Update

The follow-up clarified pricing, portal fit, payments, and onboarding risk.

The 7/10 meeting separated AR from AP, showed the Enterprise Customer Portal, clarified the QuickBooks Online integration model, and set up the PSA demo as the next decision point. The most important takeaway: Rev.io can run AR, portal payments, customer invoices, and payment reporting inside the platform, while AP remains a separate partner portal and QuickBooks Online remains the GL/P&L system.

AR is integrated; AP is separate

Customer invoicing, auto-pay, ACH/card/debit payments, settlement reporting, chargebacks, and portal activity live in Rev.io. Vendor payments/AP use a partner portal with virtual-card rebate and controls, but that is not part of the single Rev.io database.

QBO sync is one-way

Rev.io pushes customers, invoices, and payments to QuickBooks Online. Payments entered directly in QBO do not flow back into Rev.io, so AR activity needs to originate in Rev.io once live.

Portal value is now explicit

Enterprise Portal is a $1,000/month option. SouthEast needs to decide whether parent-child hierarchy, single-login access, customer-managed payments, invoice downloads, and customer-visible reports are worth implementing now.

Onboarding depends on data readiness

Rev.io said QBO data does not need to be perfectly clean before onboarding starts, but the prospect is still targeting August reconciliation with a hard September 15 deadline before full migration confidence.

Ranger MSP needs a migration path

Rev.io should provide an API data-access guide for Ranger MSP migration and clarify how historical CRM/ticketing data will be extracted and mapped.

PSA demo is the next proof point

The next demo should focus on CRM, time tracking, scheduling/dispatching, and inventory management. PSA is estimated at roughly $1,000/month for about 7 users on a month-to-month term.

Onboarding clarity from 7/10: the implementation plan includes a project manager, data migration engineer, and solutions analyst. Setup fees are split 50% due 90 days after signature and 50% due on activation. The longer pole is expected to be usage rating/CDR importer setup, preferably through API rather than CSV templates.
