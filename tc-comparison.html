T&C Comparison: Old vs. New

Old: Rev.io T&C July 15, 2025  ·  New: Schedule A/B Doc + act.rev.io/rev.io-terms-conditions (Jan 2026)

⚠️ Structural Change — This Is the First Thing to Understand

The old T&C was a single self-contained document. The new version is split across two sources — creating potential conflicts and ambiguity about which governs in a dispute:

  • Online T&Cs (act.rev.io/rev.io-terms-conditions, Jan 2026) — the master terms, can be updated by Rev.io unilaterally
  • Schedule A/B Document (Billing_Schedule_A_B) — billing-specific supplement with SLA details, liquidated damages, and pricing mechanics

The online T&Cs can change at any time without a contract amendment. Any customer signing under this structure should request that the Schedule A/B be explicitly incorporated by reference and that the online T&Cs be frozen at signing, or negotiate for a fully offline T&C document.

🔴 Changes Worse for Customers

Price Increase Cap: 5% → 7%

Worse
Old

Rev.io may increase Charges up to 5% per fiscal year. Unused increase capacity carries forward — Rev.io can retroactively catch up missed increases in future years.

New (Online T&C §3.1)

Cap increased to 7% per fiscal year. Retroactive catch-up language removed. Applies to both Initial and Renewal Terms.

Phone Support Now Costs Extra

Worse
Old

Email and telephone access to help desk included. M–F, 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM ET. Up to 2 authorized support contacts. After-hours escalation to Account Manager or VP Client Services.

New (Online T&C §4.1)

Email only in base contract. "Phone support is available for an additional fee." Support hours listed as 9am–5pm ET. No mention of authorized contact limit or after-hours escalation in online T&Cs.

"Hotlined" Removed from Active Customer Definition

Worse
Old

Active Customer = any subscriber with an active, suspended, or hotlined service in the billing month.

New (Online T&C §1.1)

Active Customer = any subscriber with an active or suspended service. Hotlined removed. If your platform tracks hotlined accounts, this changes what counts toward billing.

CDR (Call Detail Record) Definition Removed

Worse
Old

CDR explicitly defined: a computer record of a phone call. Billable CDR = any CDR retrieved or received into and processed by the Rev.io system.

New (Both Docs)

CDR definition removed entirely. If pricing is CDR-based, this creates ambiguity about what constitutes a billable CDR in a dispute. Needs to be defined in the ordering document.

Uptime SLA Weakened in Online T&Cs

Worse
Old (and New Schedule A/B)

Explicit 99.9% uptime guarantee. Credits: 1/30th of monthly fee per 60-min outage, max 20% per month. Right to terminate if 2 separate 8-hour outages occur in one calendar month.

New (Online T&C §4.3–4.4)

99.9% guarantee gone. Replaced with "commercially reasonable efforts." Credits: "sole remedy limited to SLA if applicable or as agreed in writing." Note: Schedule A/B doc retains the 99.9% SLA — but only if it's contractually controlling.

Corporate Change Notification Window Shortened

Worse
Old

Must notify Rev.io within 30 days of corporate change (acquisition, merger, reorganization). Must execute addendum within 30 days of notification to avoid suspension.

New (Online T&C §2.2)

Must notify within 10 business days. Failure = immediate material breach and automatic acknowledgement of continuation. No addendum process — stricter consequence for missing the window.

Subpoena: Rev.io May Not Notify You

Worse
Old

If Rev.io receives a subpoena for your data, Licensee reimburses Rev.io's professional time. Rev.io "may or may not inform the Licensee" depending on court direction.

New (Online T&C §3.2)

Rev.io shall notify Licensee in writing if legally permissible — an improvement in language, but the operative phrase is still "if legally permissible." Reimbursement of Rev.io's costs still required. Overall slightly better framing but same practical outcome.

🟡 Items to Watch
👁️

Online T&Cs can change without your consent. The master terms live at a URL Rev.io controls. Unlike a Schedule attached to a signed agreement, the online T&Cs could be updated by Rev.io unilaterally. Customers should push for a version freeze at signing or require 60-day notice of material changes with opt-out rights.

👁️

Authorized User definition removed from both new documents. The old T&C defined who could access the platform. The new version has no such definition in either the online T&Cs or the Schedule A/B. If per-user access becomes a billing or compliance issue, there's no definition to anchor the dispute.

👁️

Liquidated damages (95%) and reinstatement fee (20%) are in Schedule A/B — but not the online T&Cs. If a dispute arises, expect Rev.io to invoke Schedule A/B. Customers should confirm in writing which document governs in a conflict, and ensure Schedule A/B is explicitly incorporated by reference with Schedule A/B controlling on billing matters.

🟢 Changes Better for Customers

Explicit IP Indemnification by Rev.io — NEW

Better
Old

No explicit IP indemnification in the T&C.

New (Online T&C §8.4)

Rev.io will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Licensee from third-party IP infringement claims against the Licensed Products. Standard carve-outs for Licensee modifications and Licensee Data.

Explicit Liability Cap — NEW

Better
Old

No explicit cap on Rev.io's liability in the T&C.

New (Online T&C §8.3)

Rev.io's total liability capped at amounts paid in the prior 12 months. Also explicitly excludes indirect/consequential damages. Mutual cap — cuts both ways.

Non-Solicitation Clause Removed

Better
Old

Licensee could not solicit or hire any Rev.io employee during the Term or for 12 months after. Penalty: 50% of employee's starting salary.

New

Removed from both new documents. Customers are free to hire Rev.io employees without contractual penalty.

Professional Conduct Clause Removed

Better
Old

"Licensee shall at all times act in a professional and respectful manner toward Rev.io and its employees. Violation of this duty shall be a material breach of this Agreement."

New

Removed. The old clause gave Rev.io broad discretion to declare material breach based on subjective conduct standards.

Explicit Licensee Data Ownership — NEW

Better
Old

Rev.io's right to use Licensee Data was described; Licensee's ownership was implied but not stated explicitly.

New (Online T&C §10.2)

Licensee explicitly retains all right, title, and interest in Licensee Data. Rev.io's license is scoped to what's needed to provide the service only.

📋 Key Action Items
1️⃣

Confirm which document governs in a conflict. Get written confirmation that Schedule A/B controls on billing, SLA, and liquidated damages — not the online T&Cs. The 99.9% SLA and 95% liquidated damages only exist in Schedule A/B.

2️⃣

Address the CDR definition gap. If any product's pricing is CDR-based, the definition needs to be in the ordering document or addendum. The old T&C defined it; neither new document does.

3️⃣

Confirm phone support is included (or price it). The online T&Cs now list phone support as an add-on fee. If existing customers assume it's included, this is a silent service reduction. Confirm whether existing contracts are grandfathered.

4️⃣

Note the price cap increase. Old cap was 5%; new is 7%. For customers on annual contracts this is a material change — worth surfacing to customers or negotiating back to 5% if possible.

5️⃣

Lock the online T&Cs version. Any new agreement should reference a specific dated version of the online T&Cs (Jan 2026) and require 60-day written notice before Rev.io can substitute a new version.
